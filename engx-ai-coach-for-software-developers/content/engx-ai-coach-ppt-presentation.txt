We have come to the final step of the course: Final Exam. Here’re what you need to do to pass the exam:
Create a slide deck covering one of the topics from the list below, attach the deck to an email, and send it to the course coordinators at OrgEngXSoftwareDevelopersCoach@epam.com.
Once your deck if approved by the course coordinators via email, you'll be invited to the 45-min exam. 
Here’re the details on what will happen during the exam and how to prepare: 
Exam duration is 45 min. You will have 20-25 min to present one of the following topics and rest of the time will be Q&A. While getting ready for presentation, keep in mind the target audience - developers who started using GenAI 3 months ago.

Applying MCP in real (or improvised) use case (Github and Atlassian MCP servers are not allowed)
Awesome Refactoring and/or Code Review use case (it should really be an awesome one)
How to develop your own multi-agentic solution (LangChain, LangGraph, ADK etc.)
Success story of applying Coding Agent (Claude Code etc.) 
AI Security use cases
AI Trends applicable for clients
Your own topic (please contact Oleksandr Pavlovskyi or Vitalii Matviichuk in advance for topic approval)

Consider this as your presentation to client's teams. It should be interesting, engaging, relevant. You will be able to reuse those materials later during real AI Adoption program with the client. 

Presentation self-review checklist:
story corresponds to the defined audience (developers who are using GenAI tools for 3 months already)
the audience will learn new topic (not use case, but topic)
the presentation is not presales or case study style but rather a lecture on a certain with examples 
it contains problem statement, theory and practical tasks (we would love to see the code!)
presentation must contain less text and max visual to explain concept
practical part is more important than presentation (theoretical part)
proposed storyline for the presentation (this is not mandatory to follow, feel free to deviate): agenda - about you - topic intro - theory (which problems your topic solves, how if helps developers) - comparison of alternative solutions - practice (code)
The successful completion of the exam will be evaluated in a ‘Done’ – ‘Not Done’ format by the coaches considering your ability to deliver such workshops yourself in the future. You can potentially refer to the following criteria: 
Understanding of Topic: The participant should have a comprehensive understanding of the topic they're presenting. This can be measured by how well they explain the concepts, their ability to answer questions, and their use of relevant examples.
Presentation Skills: This includes clarity of speech, confidence, body language, and the ability to engage the audience. The participant should also demonstrate good time management skills by keeping the presentation within the allotted time.
Use of Visual Aids: The participant should effectively use visual aids such as PowerPoint slides, live demos, charts, or videos to enhance their presentation. These aids should be clear, relevant, and add value to the presentation.
Application of Knowledge: The participant should demonstrate how the learned concepts can be applied in real-life situations. This can be measured by the use of case studies, examples, or scenarios.
Structure of the Workshop: The participant should have a clear introduction, body, and conclusion. The content should flow logically and smoothly from one point to the next.
